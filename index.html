About Me

I am a machine learning specialist focusing on the application of data science techniques to subsurface related problems. Currently a research scientist in the Ali I. Al-Naimi Petroleum Engineering Research Center working alongside the Seismic Wave Analysis Group in KAUST, my work mainly focuses on the creation and adaptation of deep learning procedures for seismic data processing and interpretation. Previously, I worked in Equinor (née Statoil) as a data scientist implementing data driven solutions across the full value chain.

I am particularly passionate about demystifying 'Artificial Intelligence' and its potential use within the Energy sector.

Research

My current research interests span the field of: noise modelling and suppression, microseismic monitoring, and the use of deep learning for seismic signal processing and interpretation. Alongside these, I am passionate about the organisation of unstructured data and the knowledge that can be obtained from it.

Below are a selection of previous and ongoing research projects.

Self-supervised seismic data processing.

The lack of ground truth for field data poses a real challenge for deep learning applications in seismology. In this work we consider self-supervised approaches that learn directly from the noisy data itself.

Noise modelling for synthetic data generation.

Present in all field seismic data, we aim to statistically replicate noise for the generation of realistic synthetic seismic datasets. More so, by understanding noise we develop procedures for its suppression.

Microseismic detection using neural networks.

Neural networks are particularly powerful at pattern detection and therefore lend themselves naturally to the task of microseismic event detection. We have investigated both RNNs and CNNs for this purpose.



Natural language processing for O&G.

The oil and gas industry has its own lingo and as such needs its own language models. This work focuses on the combination of standard and cutting-edge NLP tools for extracting information from geoscientific texts.

Large-scale deep learning for geophysics.

Seismic data is often orders of magnitudes larger than other data types like images. To remove requirements for windowing and cropping data, we exploit distributed deep learning.

GraphDB of open seismic datasets.

The lack of openly available geoscientific datasets is a pain point for most researchers. We have open-sourced an interactive graph database detailing available seismic datasets and their key features.
